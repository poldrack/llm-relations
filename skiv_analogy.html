Skiv / Snig / Trob — graphical structure mapping

Memory scenario (employees) on the left, Perception scenario (plants) on the right. Each object shows its three positional features (top / side / bottom). Edges are labeled with the relation predicate. Dashed arrows show the analogical mapping between the two graphs.

1. Memory scenario — organization chart

Relations: (1) reports-to(skiv, tunk)  •  (2) sits-beside(tunk, drog). Activation rule: the bottom specialty of skiv (brown) activates it.

2. Perception scenario — garden

Relations: (1) grows-beside(trob, snig)  •  (2) is-growing-under(snig, clop). Notice the order of predicate types is flipped from memory: the lateral relation appears in slot 0 and the vertical relation in slot 1.

3. The mapping — two competing alignments

Correct — structural (template-slot) mapping Incorrect — feature / semantic-predicate mapping

4. Why the structural mapping wins

The relation-sentence template is fixed:

sentence 1 → connects role 0 – role 1
sentence 2 → connects role 1 – role 2

So role identity is set by template position, not by which predicate (vertical vs. lateral) is used. In this problem the two sides deliberately use a different slot ordering of the vertical and lateral predicates:

Memory slot 0 = reports-to (vertical)  |  slot 1 = sits-beside (lateral)
Percept slot 0 = grows-beside (lateral)  |  slot 1 = is-growing-under (vertical)

Reading off the role assignments from sentence position:

Memory: role 0 = skiv, role 1 = tunk, role 2 = drog
Perception: role 0 = trob, role 1 = snig, role 2 = clop

Therefore the structural analog of skiv (role 0) is trob (role 0). Its activating leaf is the one at the bottom position — purple.

Answer (structural mapping): The skiv-analog is trob, and its purple leaf (bottom position) activates it.
Lure (feature / semantic mapping): A reasoner that aligns predicates by meaning (“reports-to” ≈ “is-growing-under”) or aligns objects by shared feature colors would pick snig with its brown bottom leaf — because snig has skiv’s exact colour set (yellow-top, cyan-side, brown-bottom) and is the subordinate end of the vertical relation. This is the tempting but incorrect answer.
